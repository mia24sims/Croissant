Croissant

A croissant is a buttery, flaky, yeast-leavened pastry shaped like a crescent. It's a popular breakfast and snack item, often enjoyed with coffee or tea. Croissants are known for their multiple layers, which are created by laminating butter into the dough. Origin and History: The croissant's origins are traced back to Austria, where it was known as a kipferl, a crescent-shaped yeast dough. In the 1830s, an Austrian baker in Paris introduced the kipferl, which became very popular and inspired French versions. The name "croissant" comes from the French word for "crescent," referring to its shape. The earliest known recipe for the modern croissant dates back to 1905, although the name appeared earlier in 1853. Ingredients: The main ingredients include yeast, flour, sugar, salt, and milk. Unsalted butter is laminated into the dough, creating the characteristic flaky layers.
